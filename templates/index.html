How it works

What does this do?

It replaces the boot logo — the image shown when your iPod starts up — inside your Rockbox firmware file. That's it. Your music, settings, themes, and everything else is untouched.

View source on GitHub

What do I get?

A ZIP file containing three things:

  • rockbox.ipod — the patched firmware with your new logo
  • rockbox.ipod.original — the original, unmodified firmware
  • RESTORE.txt — step-by-step restore instructions

How do I install it?

  1. Download the ZIP and unzip it — you'll find three files inside.
  2. Connect your iPod via USB. It mounts as a drive (e.g. "IPOD").
  3. Show hidden files so you can see the .rockbox/ folder:
    • macOS: Press Cmd + Shift + . in Finder
    • Windows: In Explorer, go to View → Show → Hidden items
    • Linux: Press Ctrl + H in your file manager, or use ls -a in the terminal
  4. Copy rockbox.ipod into <iPod>/.rockbox/rockbox.ipod, replacing the existing file.
  5. Eject the iPod and reboot (hold MENU + SELECT for 6 seconds).
  6. Your new boot logo appears.

Does it affect my settings or music?

No. Rockbox stores your settings in .rockbox/config.cfg and your music is on a completely separate part of the drive. Replacing rockbox.ipod only updates the firmware — like updating an app without losing your preferences.

What if something goes wrong?

The iPod can't be bricked this way. If the firmware is rejected, the iPod just shows an error on boot. To restore:

  1. Reboot: hold MENU + SELECT for 6 seconds.
  2. Immediately hold SELECT + PLAY to enter disk mode.
  3. The iPod mounts as USB again.
  4. Copy rockbox.ipod.original from the ZIP back to <iPod>/.rockbox/rockbox.ipod (rename it).
  5. Eject and reboot. You're back to stock Rockbox.

The original Apple firmware lives on a separate partition and is never touched by any of this.

Podpal uses official Rockbox firmware from rockbox.org. No files on your iPod are modified until you copy the patched file yourself.
